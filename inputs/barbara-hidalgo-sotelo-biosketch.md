### Undergraduate Research & Early Career Turning Points
#### Motorola Biochip Systems internship — Tempe, Arizona (summer 1999)
- Barbara reports being contacted by a Motorola molecular biologist (Dr. Robert Marrero) and invited to a summer internship at Motorola Biochip Systems in Tempe, AZ—an experience she cites as both technically formative and identity-shaping.
- **Source:** Personal recollection provided by Barbara.

#### Shift toward neuroscience & vision science (UT Austin coursework)
- During a Vertebrate Physiology course, Barbara describes a pivotal shift from genetics toward neuroscience, especially the visual system—connecting EE concepts (e.g., membrane capacitance) to perceptual experience and motivating her later focus on vision and attention.
- **Source:** Personal recollection provided by Barbara.

#### Mentors and research apprenticeships (UT Austin)
- Barbara identifies Dr. Alan Cline (Dean’s Scholars leadership) as a key figure encouraging research immersion.
- Reported lab experiences:
  - Michael Domjan lab (2001–2002): learning/memory research in quail (user-provided)
  - Howard Zakon lab (2003–2004): LabVIEW software for zebrafish experiments (user-provided)
- **Source:** Personal recollection provided by Barbara.

#### Comparative RNA Lab (UT Austin) — Research Assistant as a first job after graduating from undergrad
- Barbara worked in the Comparative RNA Lab under PI Dr. Robin Gutell, conducting daily NCBI searches for newly published rRNA sequences and integrating them into the lab’s comparative database. Barbara reports continuing this work until sh.

## Research & Publications - Brief Overview
### Signature line of work: modeling eye guidance in real-world scenes
- Barbara’s early research with the MIT vision community (Oliva/Torralba ecosystem) focuses on how saliency, target features, and scene context jointly predict eye movements during object/person search.

### Flagship publication
- **Modelling search for people in 900 scenes: A combined source model of eye guidance** (Visual Cognition, 2009)
  - Authors include Krista A. Ehinger and Barbara Hidalgo-Sotelo (among others).
  - Widely cited across vision science and computational modeling.
  - **Source(s):**
