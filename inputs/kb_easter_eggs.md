## MIT Labmates — CVCL (Computational Visual Cognition Lab)

Barbara was part of MIT's Computational Visual Cognition Lab (CVCL) led by Aude Oliva, graduating in 2010. These were formative years and the lab was both intellectually rigorous and genuinely fun.

**The Easy Button:** Aude bought a Staples "Easy" button for the lab early on — the one that says "THAT was easy!" when you press it. It became a lab fixture.

**Labmates and where they are now:**

- **Talia Konkle** — Professor of Psychology at Harvard, Director of the Mind Brain Behavior Initiative, Kempner Institute Associate Faculty. She and George Alvarez co-direct the VisionLab at Harvard. Known for research on how the brain organizes visual knowledge, object size perception, and real-world scene representation. At MIT, she did the visual framing experiments (sketching objects on different-sized paper) and co-led the Massive Memory and Hybrid Images work with Tim, George, and Aude.

- **Timothy Brady** — Professor of Psychology at UC San Diego, Vision and Memory Lab. The Massive Memory paper with Talia, George, and Aude (PNAS 2008) showed people can recall specific details of briefly seen images — one of the most-cited papers from the lab. Tim is a major figure in visual working memory and long-term memory research.

- **Michelle Greene** — Assistant Professor of Psychology at Barnard College (Columbia University). NSF CAREER award recipient. Studies scene-level visual perception, including properties like "Openness" and "Navigability." Recently published research on socioeconomic biases in AI scene classification systems. She calls herself a "vision nerd."

- **Krista Ehinger** — Barbara's co-first-author on the 900 Scenes paper.

- **Soojin Park** — now Assistant Professor of Cognitive Science at Johns Hopkins University.

- **Olivier Joubert** — labmate in CVCL.
